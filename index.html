Biography of saint Augustine of Hippo

    The church patron saint is St Augustine of Hippo. He was born on 13th November 354 at Tagaste (North Africa). He was a great theologian and his parents were Patricius and Monica. The father was a pagan official at Tagaste. His mother was a devoted and committed Christian. He was a prudent student at Carthage. As a youth he used his life being a Manichaeism heresy who believed that all matters is evil and also having into an illicit attachment with a woman who became his mistress for the next 15 years.

    His conversion came after reading the bible Romans 13:13-14. Through the mother St. Monica prayer and fasting, Monica tried in vain to persuade a visiting Bishop who had himself passed the Manichean errors to lead her son back to the truth. Then Augustine decided to teach rhetoric in Rome. His mother St. Monica wished to accompany him but he deceived her and sailed off.

    From home he moved on to Milan where he obtained a professorship. His mother Monica followed him there where she befriended the great Bishop of Milan St. Ambrose who instructed and helped Augustine to come into the church at Easter 387AD. Having at last realized that only through Jesus Christ could he find the way to truth and eternal salvation, Augustine at peace with God, himself and the world went to live saintly life. His mother and his son having died soon after his baptism and conversion.

    Augustine then returned to Africa where he established a monastery for himself and his followers and led a life of apostolic poverty, prayer, study and active charity among the poor. After three years he was through popular insistence ordained as a priest and at the age of 42 years he became the Bishop of Hippo.

    Laboring for other 34 years he revitalized the entire African church becoming the patriarch of monasticism. He lived a strict community life with his priest. St Augustine is one of the world’s greatest thinkers as a theologian and philosopher. He is also outstanding among the church fathers. His memory of his own youthful kept him always humble.

    St Augustine died of fever on 28th August 430 while the vandal invaders were besieging Hippo. He is honored as the patron of theologians’ printers.
