History of PMC

The Association was founded on 19th May 1843 by a French Bishop called De Forbin Janson. PMC also known as HOLY CHILDHOOD; Bishop de Forbin Janson put the Association under the Patronage of the Child Jesus, hence the name HOLY CHILDHOOD. He wanted the children to be inspired by the life of the Child Jesus. They learned from the Child Jesus how to live a life of obedience to their Parents and other people as they try to imitate Him. Bishop de Forbin Janson visited other Missionaries where were working in China, and there, he learned how children belonging to poor families were facing difficulty situations. He was used by God to save the children of china who were dying without baptism. He involved children to become his helper in fulfilling the call that he had received from God.

It's Motto

Was purposely formed for catholic child to help less fortunate children, hence its motto“CHILDREN HELPING CHILDREN”. Through their motto, children are dedicated to pray and offer financial support to other children all over the world.

It's Objective

The main objective of PMC is to make our catholic children get to know and understand our catholic Faith at an early age. It’s also meant to train children how to share and help the less fortunate with materials and prayers. Generally, it is meant to strengthen our children’s Faith.

It's Symbols

  1. Membership card
  2. The Bible
  3. Cross
  4. Missionary Rosary

NOTE: The Rosary has 5 different colours to represent the 5 continents

  1. Green – Africa
  2. Red – America
  3. White – Europe
  4. Blue – Oceania
  5. Yellow – Asia
  1. The Flag – Which has three colours
  2. Yellow – represent maturity of children as they grow as Jesus did
  3. White –Reminds children to observe and keep Purity
  4. Red – Reminds children the zeal they should have to spread the gospel (even be ready to shed blood)
