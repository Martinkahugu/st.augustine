A brief history of the curch

    St Augustine Catholic Church History goes back from Ruaraka Parish to Holy Mary Mother of God parish and eventually Christ The King Kimbo Parish. As a result of the challenges experienced during the 1997/98 El- Nino rains, Christians who lived across the railway line agreed to source and buy land in anticipation of building a church. A committee of small Christian communities was floated by Mr. Joseph Maina, and these comprised St Jude ,St Augustine(now St Ambrose),St Francis of Assisi, St Matthias ,St Cecilia ,St Joseph, St.Veronica and St John the Baptist who later joined Holy Mary Mother of God as they were very close to the road. These S.C.C used to meet at Gabriel Muriithi’s house to plan ahead and in their first meeting of 8th February 1998 a total sh.78,000 was raised.

    The Late Fr. David Njuguna (R.I.P) called the Christians after a plot he had been promised by Ngara Mucokaniririria Land Company materialized. Two more plots were given by the company for public utility courtesy of the Late Joyce Wachira.

    The money that had been collected was used to put up a first mabati church. Christians were very committed and Fr. Francis Wairegi, the then parish priest, Ruaraka Parish under which Holy Mary mother of God and Githurai Kimbo were outstations, blessed the mabati church. Christians used to get Mass on a fortnight basis on Saturday, but they held prayers every Wednesday evening.

In October 1999, Holy Mary Mother of God parish was created with Fr. Luigi as the parish priest assisted by Fr. Peter Gullen. St .Augustine and Christ the King were outstations but shortly after Christ the King Parish was created and St. Augustine became an outstation of Christ the King Parish. Fr.Peter Gullen became the parish priest. The construction of the current church building started in……….. and the ground breaking was done by Fr. Komu the then Parish priest of Umoja Parish. The church was blessed by His Grace Bishop David Kamau upon completion in……… the church confirmed to experience tremendous growth and bought several plots for expansion and in 2011,St. Veronica Church was born of St. Augustine. St. Augustine Christians continued to support the church and can be witnessed from the development records, there is room for further growth.
